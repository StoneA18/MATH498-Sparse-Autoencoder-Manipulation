The sterile white walls of the hospital seemed to hum with a quiet, almost mournful energy. Dr. Anya Sharma, her face etched with the lines of countless sleepless nights, sat opposite the patient, a worn leather chair creaking a rhythmic lullaby. Her eyes, usually bright with a patient's enthusiasm, were clouded with a weary sadness.

"You're feeling…unsettled," she said, her voice a low, steady hum. "Like you're being watched."
