The old lighthouse keeper, Silas, stood on the craggy, windswept cliff, his weathered face etched with the stories of countless storms and the relentless rhythm of the sea. He had seen it all, from the blinding flashes of the dawn to the mournful cries of the whales, and he knew the language of the ocean. He knew the secrets whispered by the waves, the whispers of the wind, the whispers of the stars.

He had been a keeper of the light for fifty years
