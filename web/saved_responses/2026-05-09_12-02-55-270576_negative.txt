The old lighthouse stood sentinel against the churning grey sea, its beam a lonely eye in the perpetual twilight. For decades, it had watched the tides rise and fall, the sun dip and rise, the storms rage and rage. It had seen the laughter of children, the tears of lovers, the silent screams of the lost.

One stormy night, a young woman named Elara, her face etched with worry, slipped into the lighthouse. She was searching for her father, a man she had lost
