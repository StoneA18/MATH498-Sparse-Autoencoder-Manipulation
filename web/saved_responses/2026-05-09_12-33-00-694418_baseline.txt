The old lighthouse keeper, Silas, stood on the craggy, windswept cliff, the beam of his lantern cutting through the grey gloom. He’d seen countless storms, felt the relentless kiss of the sea, and the chilling silence of the night. He’d lived a life of quiet routine, a life of duty, a life that felt like a prison. 

One stormy night, a shadow fell upon the lighthouse. A monstrous wave, larger than any he’d ever seen
